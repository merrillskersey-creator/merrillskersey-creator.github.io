My Travel Philosophy

I've always believed that stepping outside your comfort zone — whether that's into a new country, a dense forest, or a mountain summit — shapes you in ways no classroom ever can. Travel is my greatest teacher.

From the red rock canyons of Utah to the ancient cities of Europe, every journey has left its mark on how I think, communicate, and lead. This page is a window into that world.

"Not all those who wander are lost." — J.R.R. Tolkien

My Top Travel Experiences

  1. Hiking the Narrows — Zion National Park, Utah
    • Wading through the Virgin River for miles
    • Slot canyon walls hundreds of feet high
    • Sunrise start to beat the crowds
  2. Backpacking in the Swiss Alps
    • Traversed the Via Alpina multi-day route
    • Remote mountain huts with stunning glacier views
    • Met travelers from 15+ countries
  3. Exploring Patagonia, Argentina
    • Torres del Paine W Trek
    • Calafate glacier walks at El Calafate
    • Wildlife: condors, pumas, and guanacos
  4. Road Trip: Pacific Coast Highway, California
    • Drove from SF to San Diego over 6 days
    • Big Sur camping under the stars
    • Elephant seals at Piedras Blancas

Essential Travel Gear

After years of trial and error, here's what always makes it into my pack:

  • Osprey Atmos AG 65L backpack — unbeatable suspension for long carries
  • Merino wool base layers — warm, breathable, and odor-resistant
  • Lightweight titanium cookset — saves weight without sacrificing function
  • Sony A6400 mirrorless camera — compact but produces stunning landscape shots
  • Offline maps downloaded via Maps.me — works with no cell service
  • Water purification tablets as backup — always pack them, rarely need them
